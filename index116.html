Micro-sandblasting

Micro sandblasting uses air and micro particles to create structures on a micrometric scale. With this technique it is also possible to create holes when either the substrate is very thin (foil) or the method is applied longer. The geometry of created structures is defined by masks.

Chemical process

A surface can be modified by a chemical treatment. An example is removing native oxygen from the surface of a silicon wafer. In this case ammonium fluoride is put to the surface of the substrate and reacts with the oxygen.

micro-milling

Micro milling is a mechanical way to structure a substrate. It works like milling, but on a microscopic scale. The material is removed by a fast rotating cutter. By moving this tool, the structure is created on the substrate.
